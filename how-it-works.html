How CrossyBot Works

Markets often move in trends‚Äîsome short, some long‚Äîand one way to spot them is through moving averages.
CrossyBot uses this to help automate it's trade entries and exits.

A moving average (MA) smooths out price data by calculating and tracking an average price over a specific time period. It's used to filter out noise and highlight the direction and strength of trends in price data.

Moving averages are foundational tools in technical analysis. They help traders:
‚Ä¢ Identify trend direction
‚Ä¢ Spot potential reversals
‚Ä¢ Make decisions based on objective, calculated levels instead of emotions

An Exponential Moving Average (EMA) is a type of moving average which gives more weight to recent price data, making it more responsive to current price action.
A crossover occurs when a short-term EMA crosses above (bullish) or below (bearish) a longer-term EMA, signaling potential trend reversals or momentum shifts.

Crossover strategies are time-tested tools for trend-following . They're simple, effective, and adaptive across market conditions. By focusing on moving average relationships, they remove noise and reduce emotional decision-making.

Crosses for Trade Opening

CrossyBot first identifies the larger trend using a higher timeframe EMA pair (1D to 5M).
This filters trades based on the prevailing direction, reducing false signals during choppy or range-bound markets.

Once a trend bias is established, a lower timeframe (4H to 1M) EMA crossover is used to time entries.
This allows for sharper execution while still aligning with the broader trend.

Trend from higher timeframe = directional bias
Trigger from lower timeframe = signal to enter

Example:
1H chart shows 50 EMA > 200 EMA (bullish bias)
5M chart triggers 10 EMA crossing above 20 EMA ‚Üí Enter spot trade

Using dual timeframes aligns trade entries with higher probability setups. It reduces whipsaws and improves conviction.

Crosses for Trade Closing

On a lower timeframe, when EMAs cross against the open trade direction, CrossyBot checks if the trade is in profit.

If it is, the trade closes automatically. If not, it stays open until closed manually or a stop loss has been hit.

Auto trade closing is always active and picks up any new balances or positions as and when they appear.

Trades will be closed on profitable moving average crosses on the lower timeframe against the direction of your trade.

To disable this functionality, you can either:

  • Add the pair to excluded pairs (where automations are skipped for that pair)
  • Pause all automations entirely (where automations are skipped for all pairs)

Longer timeframes = fewer signals, higher reliability
